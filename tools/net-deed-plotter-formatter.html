The format, in plain terms. Net Deed Plotter reads each straight course as Ndd.mmssE distance: quadrant, degrees, a period, then minutes and seconds as four digits, the second quadrant, a space, and the distance. So "North 22 degrees 13 minutes 10 seconds East, 200.7 feet" becomes N22.1310E 200.7. Varas keep a V on the distance. Curves are not parsed, so enter any curve by hand. Get the software at deedplotter.com, and see the McCracken Net Deed Plotter cheat sheet on the Tools page.

Working from a scanned deed? This tool needs typed or clean text. Scanned county deeds with rough OCR will not parse reliably. An AI deed reader that handles messy scans is in the works.
