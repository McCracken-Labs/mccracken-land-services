Excel or Proprietary Software for Ownership Reports?

By Michael McCracken • March 24, 2026 • 1 min read

Excel gives you control and a visible audit trail. Land systems give you consistency across properties. A look at what landmen actually trust when deadlines are tight.

As a landman, do you prefer to build your leasehold and mineral ownership reports in Excel or in proprietary software? Which system was your favorite?

Many still rely on Excel because it offers direct control over calculations and makes the logic easy to see and audit. It adapts well to irregular title issues, blanket assignments, partial interests, and county specific details, even though it can be time consuming to maintain as projects grow.

Others have moved toward dedicated land systems or custom software that centralize data and apply consistent logic across multiple properties. These tools can reduce manual rework when ownership changes, but they often come with setup costs and rigid structures that do not always align with how title is analyzed in the field.

Some teams end up using both, keeping data in a system while delivering reports in Excel or Word since those formats are still the most familiar for attorneys, clients, and examiners.

I am interested in how this plays out in real work, based on what you actually trust when accuracy matters and deadlines are tight.
